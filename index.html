Accomplishments.

A collection of his accomplishments throughout his NBA career.

2015 Championship

The first championship in 40 years for the Golden State Warriors, Curry was able to establish himself as a transformative player of the game.

2017 Championship

With the help of Kevin Durant, Curry and the Golden State Warriors were able to secure their second championship in three years after a very dominant season.

2018 Championship

The Cleveland Cavaliers vs Golden State Warriors rivalry continues as Curry and his teammates are able to secure back-to-back titles and their thrid championship in 4 years. Curry scored 37 points in the final and Kevin Durant wins his second NBA Finals Trophy.

2022 Championship

Curry's thrist to prove his dominance, extraordinary leadership, and clutch performances lifted the Golden State Warriors to their fourth title in eight years, solidifying his legacy as one of the game's greatest. He finally won the NBA Finals MVP Award.

MVP/Finals MVP

Curry won the NBA Most Valuable Player Award in the 2014-2015 and 2015-2016 seasons. His MVP award in the 2015-2016 was historic because it was the first (and only so far) time that an MVP was chosen unanimously. Additionally, he was the Finals MVP of the 2021-2022.

Leading Three-Point Scorer

On December 14, 2021, Steph Curry broke the all-time 3-point record previously held by Ray Allen (2,973 three-pointers).

Olympic Gold Medal

His late but impactful contribution in the final game is what ultimately secured the 2024 Olympic gold medal for the USA.

Golden State Dynasty

Alongside Klay Thompson and Draymond Green, Curry revolutionized the Golden State Warriors franchise winning them 4 championships in 8 years.

Family

Steph Curry's family is full of athletes. His father, Dell Curry, is a former NBA player. His mother, Sonya Curry, is a former volleyball player. Steph's younger brother Seth is also an NBA player and sister Sydel is currently a volleyball player and content creator.

Steph Curry met Ayesha when they were teenagers in 2003. They reconnected in 2008 and got married in 2011. As of right now, they have been married for 16 years and brought 4 kids to this world. The oldest, their daughter named Riley, was born in 2012 and took after her grandmother and aunt as she has decided to use her athletic predisposition to explore the game of volleyball. Their second daughter, Riley, who was born in 2015, has focused more on pursuing dance. Next came Canon, their only son, who was born in 2018 and lastly, they had another daughter, named Caius Chai, who was born in 2024.

Draymond Green

After being drafted in 2012, Draymond Green has developed to be one of the best forwards in the Golden State Franchise, even winning NBA Defensive Player of the Year in the 2016-2017 season. Curry and Green's connection has played a crucial role in the success of the team.

Klay Thompson

Also know for his extraordinary ability at the three-point line, Thompson and Curry have developed a long lasting bond in the 13 years they played together. Probably the most dangerous pair in the Golden State Dynasty, this duo was honored with the nickname "Splash Brothers." No other team stands a chance when this two are perfectly in sync.

Andre Iguodala

A very wise player and part of the Golden State champions, Andre Iguodala was an essential piece of the puzzle that was the success of the team which ultimately led to their championships. In 2019, Iguodala published an autobiography of how basketball carreer affected his entire life and how it formed and guided him to his success with the Golden State Warriors.
